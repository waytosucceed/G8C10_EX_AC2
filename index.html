Exponents

Simplifying Algebraic Expressions

Exponents play a crucial role in reducing complex algebraic expressions.

  • Exponents allow us to combine terms with the same base by adding or subtracting the powers.

Example:
x3 . x2 = x3 + 2 = x5


  • Exponents simplify distribution in expressions.

Example:
(2x3)2 = 22 . (x3)2 = 4x6


  • Exponents simplify factoring by grouping terms with common bases (We will study this later as a separate topic).

Solving Linear Equations

Exponents help handle equations involving powers or roots during simplifications.

  • Eliminating Exponents Using Roots: To solve equations like:

x2 = 16


  • Exponent Rules for Simplifying Linear Terms:

Example: 3x1/2 + 50 = 20:
3x1/2 = 15
x1/2 = 5
x = 25


  • Exponential Growth or Decay in Equations:

Simplify equations where growth or decay follows exponential patterns.
Example: 2x = 32:
2x = 25
x = 5
